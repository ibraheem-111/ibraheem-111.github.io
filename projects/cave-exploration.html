Autonomous Cave Exploration Drone (GNSS-Denied)

Frontier-based autonomous mapping of a GNSS-denied cave with a PX4/Gazebo quadrotor — RGB-D SLAM, external-vision EKF2 fusion, and reactive lidar avoidance.

Summary

Apr 2026 - May 2026 | Arizona State University

Built a full simulation stack for autonomous mapping of an unknown cave where GNSS and compass are unavailable. A PX4/Gazebo quadrotor with RGB-D sensing, 2D lidar, and a front spotlight estimates its own motion visually, explores toward map frontiers, avoids walls reactively, and returns home — all without a global position source.

What I built

  • A PX4/Gazebo cave drone equipped with an RGB-D camera, 2D lidar, IMU, and a front spotlight for the dark environment.
  • A GNSS-denied PX4 estimator setup: EKF2 configured for external vision, taking position and heading from onboard perception instead of GPS.
  • A ROS-to-PX4 odometry bridge converting RTAB-Map RGB-D odometry into PX4 VehicleOdometry, including the ENU/FLU → NED/FRD coordinate-frame handling.
  • A ROS 2 autonomy node handling takeoff, cave entry, frontier exploration, sampled local path selection, reactive lidar avoidance, return behavior, and RViz marker visualization.
  • CSV logging of pose, battery, coverage area, travel distance, and RTAB-Map feature counts for run-to-run comparison.

System

Results

  • End-to-end GNSS-denied autonomous mapping working in simulation: PX4 SITL ↔ Gazebo ↔ ROS 2 ↔ RTAB-Map VIO ↔ EKF2 external-vision fusion ↔ frontier exploration.
  • ~2,900 lines of original Python, anchored by a 2,400+ line offboard autonomy state machine.
  • Drafted an IEEE-format report covering the system architecture and the GNSS-denied estimator design.
  • Honest scope: this is a course-project demonstration. Known limitations remain in local-planner robustness and obstacle avoidance — captured in a research roadmap toward a publishable contribution.
