Quadruped RL with 2-DoF Spine

Current one-month challenge: hardware modification + RL control for tight cornering

February 2026 - Present | Tempe, AZ

What I built

  • Modified a Unitree Go2 by introducing a 2-joint spine mechanism.
  • Developed an RL policy for navigating tight corners in a narrow hallway setup.
  • Designed action and observation spaces using IMU, joint states, and relative obstacle/goal pose.
  • Trained and iterated policies in Isaac Sim while tuning environment dynamics and constraints.

System architecture

Policy Training Loop

Isaac Sim Environment
  -> Sensor Pipeline (IMU + joint states + relative pose)
  -> Observation Vector Construction
  -> RL Policy (actor/critic)
  -> Action Commands to Quadruped Controller
  -> Reward + Constraints (cornering stability, progress, collisions)
  -> Logging and Evaluation

Key engineering decisions

  • Chose a short, challenge-focused scope to iterate quickly on policy design and reward shaping.
  • Used simulation-first training in Isaac Sim to accelerate experiments before hardware-heavy validation.
  • Included relative obstacle/goal pose in observations to improve behavior around tight turns.
  • Balanced agility and stability by tuning rewards for progress, smoothness, and failure penalties.

Results (in progress)

  • Baseline policy can complete hallway turns in simulation without collision under nominal settings.
  • Current focus: robustness against dynamics variation and tighter corner radii.
  • TODO: add final challenge metrics (success rate, average episode return, corner completion time).
